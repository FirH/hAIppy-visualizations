📊 Logistic Regression in 3D — Two Features

Now the model has two inputs: study hours and sleep hours. The model learns how both affect your chance of passing and draws a probability surface in 3D.

💡 Why 3D? When you have two features, the probability becomes a surface instead of a curve. Imagine a hilly landscape where high ground indicates likeliness to pass, while low ground indicates likeliness to fail. The flat middle (50%) becomes a line instead of a point, which is the decision boundary. Rotate the 3D chart to explore it!
